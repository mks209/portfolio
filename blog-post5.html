Are computers our new copywriters?

By Mie Sorensen / November 19, 2020 / 6:20pm

As Artificial intelligence creeps closer to mimicking and besting our humble human capabilities, it turns its robot head towards copywriting.

Not long ago writers seemed adamant that the cold, metal machinery of computer intelligence would never be a match for the creative and nuanced human mind.

But developments in natural language processing have seen companies like Persado and Phrasee emerge as digital copywriting systems that claim to produce better results than their human counterparts.

UK ecommerce firm Wowcher found that using Phrasee for social media copy led to a 31% reduction in the cost per lead.

Phrasee also claims to have produced a 35% increase in the open rate of emails for online marketplace Gumtree.

So, in the midst of this blossoming relationship between artificial intelligence and marketing, copywriters are left wondering… will these robots make them redundant?

It’s almost certain that these technologies will change the role of marketers but I’d like to think it’s for the better.

In digital marketing, writing new copy for every email subject line, push notification, Facebook ad and so forth can become time-consuming and tedious while taking up valuable resources.

If AI can give helping hand, then marketers can focus more on ensuring the quality of content and on implementing innovative strategies.

AI also creates variants of copy much faster than humans, allowing for more efficient A/B testing while acting as a tool for understanding and improving copywriting rather than replacing it.

And despite these ongoing advancements, I don’t believe AI will ever best the minds behind the most iconic copy, from the likes of “Because you’re worth it” to the unmistakeable “Just Do it”.
