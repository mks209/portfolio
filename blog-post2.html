Don't be too pushy with your push notifications

By Mie Sorensen / November 18, 2020 / 7:30pm

We are living in the age of never-ending push notifications.

Instant gratification messages pop-up on your device almost like a text from a friend, except instead of coming from a person it’s sent by an app on your mobile or web browser.

I’ve gathered 3 tips to help you use push notifications to drive traffic and promote positive engagement.

We want to avoid that dreaded feeling of constant annoyance that comes with badly designed notification strategies.

1. Think about your Timing

By analysing data about your target consumers' behaviour and mobile use, you can make sure your notifications reach them when they are primed to respond. Consider when the user may have time to engage with the notification - don’t send alerts at night when most people are asleep!


2. Make sure it’s meaningful

Like any marketing material, consumers will respond differently to different messages. It’s important to segment your notifications and personalise the message so that it is specific and relevant to the person who receives it. It should also be useful in some way; don’t nag someone to come back to your app, no one likes desperation. The Netflix mobile app is a great example of meaningful personalisation: the streaming service uses individualised data to send reminders of new seasons for show’s you previously watched and also gives the occasional recommendation based on your activity history and preferences.


3. Be careful with frequency, don’t send too many too often.

Having instant access to consumers is a privilege. Marketers need to remember that consumers are opting-in to receive these messages and can just as easily opt-out if we aggressively spam them with notifications.
