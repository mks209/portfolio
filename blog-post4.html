Hey! Did you forget something in your cart?

By Mie Sorensen / November 16, 2020 / 6:05pm

You know that moment when your scrolling through Facebook and suddenly that pair of shoes you were going buy in your last online shopping sesh pop up?

After browsing for hours, you had found the perfect pair of boots and eagerly threw them into your digital shopping cart.

But a generous dose of buyer’s remorse (and whole lot of common sense about your student budget) quickly jumped into your head!

You became one of the 69.5% of online shoppers who abandon their cart.

Except then… two days later a Facebook ad pops up to remind you of those beautiful boots you “left behind” and offers a 20% discount!

Perhaps you and the oots are meant to be after all?

This all-too-common scenario demonstrates the power of retargeting - the process of marketing to someone who’s already visited your website with the aim of enticing them back to make purchase.

Facebook is just one of the tools that allows marketers to use analytics and tracking technologies to retarget customers.

The ads on Facebook use what’s called the “Facebook Pixel”. This is a code embedded into websites which uses cookies to connect a consumer’s online behaviour to their Facebook profile.

By using tracking tools like the Facebook Pixel and pairing them with insights from data, marketers can create personalized communications that bring consumers back to the firm.

They may even reunite a consumer with that lonely, abandoned cart they left behind.

Ultimately, retargeting can benefit everyone: consumers might get a discount or free shipping on a product they have expressed interest in, and brands are able to secure sales that could have been lost otherwise.
